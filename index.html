About Me

I grew up in Washington State and I love the cold beaches of the Pacific Northwest. For my undergraduate degree, I attended Brigham Young University in Utah. I was always my family's tech support but I did not know what coding was until I took an introductory computer science class on a whim my freshman year. I liked that coding let me build things that solved problems. Computer Science gave me a structure and platform to bring ideas to life.

While at BYU, I had lots of opportunities to explore my broad interests. One of my favorite classes was a lecture series on entrepreneurship which led me to study abroad at Oxford as part of a Social Enterprise seminar. I also had the opportunity to intern with Thermo Fisher Scientific in Pleasanton, California. I also enjoyed classes about software engineering, algorithms, user experience design, and statistical machine learning.

When I am not coding, I love to knit, travel, visit art museums, and watch videos of Corgis on the internet.
